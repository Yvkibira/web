Side Hustles for students in kenya

As a student, balancing academics, social life, and financial responsibilities can be challenging. One has to get creative and explore some ways to make money. With the technologies advancing there has never been a better time for students to explore side hustles that offer flexibility, convenience, and extra income. In this article, we will explore more than 14 side hustle opportunities tailored specifically for students.

Students can utilize their free time, bicycles, or motorcycles to join these platforms as delivery partners. With the flexibility to choose their own working hours, students can earn money while zipping around the city, delivering meals and groceries to customers.   14. Selling Customized Crocs


    15. Customization is a popular trend, and selling customized Crocs can be a lucrative side hustle.   15. Selling Customized Shirts


    16. Customized shirts offer endless possibilities for creativity. Students can design and sell shirts with personalized slogans, artwork, or graphics. They can target specific niches such as school clubs, sports teams, or special events. Promoting their designs through social media and collaborating with local influencers can help increase visibility and sales.

  16. Sell Phone and Computer Stickers


    17. Phone and computer stickers are popular accessories among students. They provide an opportunity for students to showcase their creativity by designing unique stickers or curating popular designs. Students can create sticker sets with themes like pop culture, motivational quotes, or aesthetic artwork. They can sell these stickers through their own online store, social media platforms, or even partner with local retailers. Utilizing low-cost printing and packaging options will help maintain affordability for buyers.

  17. Buying Essential Items at Wholesale Prices


    18. Another side hustle idea for students is buying essential items like bread, flour, or wheat at wholesale prices and selling them at retail prices to fellow students. By establishing relationships with local suppliers or wholesalers, students can negotiate better prices and offer competitive rates to their peers. Promoting their services through word-of-mouth, social media, or distributing flyers on campus can help generate awareness.
